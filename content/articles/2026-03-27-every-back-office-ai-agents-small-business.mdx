If you've ever found yourself drowning in receipts at month-end, scrambling to check whether your leave policy is still compliant, or trying to build a cash-flow forecast in a spreadsheet at 10pm — you're not alone. Back-office admin is one of the biggest time drains for small business owners, and it rarely feels like work that moves the business forward. A San Francisco startup called Every has just launched something that directly targets that problem.

## What Every Has Built

Every is an all-in-one back-office platform for small businesses. This week it launched three AI agents designed to take over the administrative work that typically falls on the owner's plate — or gets outsourced to a bookkeeper, accountant, or HR consultant at considerable cost.

The three agents are:

- **AI CFO** — handles real-time financial modelling and cash-flow analysis, giving you a live picture of your business finances without you needing to build it yourself.
- **AI Bookkeeper** — automates transaction categorisation and month-end reconciliation, learning your business's patterns over time to become more accurate with each cycle.
- **AI CHRO** — monitors HR compliance across hiring, terminations, time-off approvals, and benefits changes, scanning current regulations and flagging risks before they become expensive problems.

## Why the Unified Platform Matters

The smart part of Every's approach isn't just the individual agents — it's that they all run on the same platform, which integrates banking, payroll, HR, benefits, accounting, and taxes into a single system.

Most businesses currently run these things across separate tools: Xero or MYOB for accounting, a separate payroll system, another HR platform, and so on. The AI agents in those siloed tools can only see part of the picture. Every's agents, by contrast, have access to the full context of your business — so the AI CFO can factor payroll obligations into cash-flow projections, and the AI CHRO can flag a hiring decision that has cost implications.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

For a small retail or service business, the day-to-day difference might look like this: instead of spending Sunday evening reconciling last week's transactions and chasing receipts, your AI Bookkeeper has already categorised everything and flagged the two items it wasn't sure about. Your AI CFO has updated your three-month cash-flow forecast based on the week's actuals. Your AI CHRO has sent you a note that a part-time employee's contract is approaching a threshold that triggers an obligation under the Fair Work Act.

That's work that would otherwise take you several hours — or cost you in consulting fees.

## What This Means for Sunshine Coast Businesses

The Sunshine Coast has a high concentration of small businesses — hospitality, trades, retail, health, and professional services — where the owner is often also the finance manager, HR manager, and operations manager. Hiring specialists for each of these functions isn't practical at the size most local businesses operate.

Tools like Every represent a meaningful shift in what's affordable and accessible. The catch to watch for is that Every is a US-based platform, which means some compliance features (particularly around HR and payroll) will be built for US regulations, not Australian ones. Before adopting any AI-powered compliance tool, it's worth checking that it has Australian-specific knowledge — or confirming that you'll maintain a human review layer for anything touching Fair Work, superannuation, or state-based payroll obligations.

That said, the financial modelling and bookkeeping functions are far more transferable, and the model itself — a unified AI platform that sees your whole business — is worth watching closely. Australian equivalents are likely not far behind.

If you're currently paying for multiple separate tools or spending significant time on back-office admin, now is a good time to map out exactly what that's costing you. The alternatives are improving fast.
