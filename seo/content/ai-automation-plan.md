## 5. Three Content Angle Options

### Option A — "The Tired Owner" (Problem/Pain Angle)
Lead with exhaustion. "You started your business to do the work you love, not to answer emails at 10pm." Emotional hook targeting burned-out SME owners. Positions AI automation as relief, not complexity.

### Option B — "The Competitive Edge" (Opportunity Angle)
Lead with FOMO. "Your competitors are already automating. Here's how to catch up — and get ahead." Data-driven, cites Australian SMB adoption stats. Works well for growth-minded business owners.

### Option C — "The Local Expert" (Trust/Authority Angle)
Lead with locality and expertise. "Sunshine Coast businesses deserve an AI partner who understands the local market." Lean into community identity, face-to-face support, and regional specificity.

### Recommendation: **Option A — "The Tired Owner"**

**Rationale:** The project brief is explicit that the target reader is a non-technical owner "who understands they need to grow but doesn't want to manage complex tech themselves." That profile is driven by pain (time, missed revenue, overwhelm) more than opportunity. Option A meets them where they are emotionally, which lowers resistance and builds trust before any service is mentioned. Options B and C work better as *supporting sections* within the page — not as the primary hook. Option B's competitive framing suits a secondary H2; Option C's local trust signals suit the "Why XentnexAI" section.
